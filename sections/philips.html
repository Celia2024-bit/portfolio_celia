Experience · 2016 – 2024

Philips Lighting

Senior Software Developer · Shanghai, China · C

Large-scale IoT lighting systems — hundreds of devices, real-time data interaction, BLE and Zigbee stacks. A persistent problem was firmware bloat: as we added more lighting modes and configurations, the firmware grew, and OTA performance suffered badly.

The fix was architectural. We co-designed a "Cloud Template + Local Logic Engine" framework — each mode became a cloud-hosted template the device could download and convert into behaviour dynamically. A default template baked into firmware meant only differences needed to be transmitted. OTA became lighter and far more flexible.

I also led a full chipset migration from Broadcom to Silicon Labs — navigating unstable SDKs, unreliable vendor support, and SDK updates requiring constant re-merging of custom code. Delivered on schedule, reducing per-unit chip costs by 25%.

When our software architect left, I stepped into the role — translating customer requirements, coordinating across hardware, software, and marketing, and making complex technical trade-offs legible to non-technical stakeholders.
